<b>Leo Gordon Laporte</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;p&#39; in &#39;pie&#39;">p</span><span title="/ɔːr/: &#39;ar&#39; in &#39;war&#39;">ɔːr</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born November 29, 1956)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is the host of <a href="/wiki/The_Tech_Guy" title="The Tech Guy">The Tech Guy</a> weekly radio show <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and a host on <a href="/wiki/TWiT.tv" title="TWiT.tv">TWiT.tv</a>, an Internet <a href="/wiki/Podcast" title="Podcast">podcast</a> network focusing on technology. He is also a former TV technology host (1998–2008) and technology author (1995, 2002–2006).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leo_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Laporte, the son of a <a href="/wiki/Geologist" title="Geologist">geologist</a>, studied Chinese history at <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> before dropping out in his junior year to pursue his career in radio broadcasting,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> where his early on-air names were Dave Allen and Dan Hayes.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He began his association with computers with his first <a href="/wiki/Home_computer" title="Home computer">home computer</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Atari_8-bit_family" title="Atari 8-bit family">Atari 400</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_and_radio">Television and radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leo_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Television and radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Laporte has worked on technology-related broadcasting projects, including <i>Dvorak on Computers</i> in January 1991 (co-hosted with technology writer <a href="/wiki/John_C._Dvorak" title="John C. Dvorak">John C. Dvorak</a>), and <i>Laporte on Computers</i> on <a href="/wiki/KGO_(AM)" title="KGO (AM)">KGO Radio</a> and <a href="/wiki/KSFO_(AM)" class="mw-redirect" title="KSFO (AM)">KSFO</a> in San Francisco.<sup id="cite_ref-Wired_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wired-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
In 1997, Laporte was awarded a Northern California Emmy for his role as <a href="/wiki/Dev_Null" title="Dev Null">Dev Null</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Motion-capture_acting" title="Motion-capture acting">motion capture</a> character on the <a href="/wiki/MSNBC" title="MSNBC">MSNBC</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Site" title="The Site">The Site</a></i>.
</p>
In 1998, Laporte created and co-hosted <i><a href="/wiki/The_Screen_Savers" title="The Screen Savers">The Screen Savers</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and the original version of <i>Call for Help</i> on the <a href="/wiki/Cable_television" title="Cable television">cable</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satellite_television" title="Satellite television">satellite</a> network <a href="/wiki/ZDTV" class="mw-redirect" title="ZDTV">ZDTV</a> (later <i>TechTV</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
Laporte was the host of the daily television show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lab_with_Leo_Laporte" title="The Lab with Leo Laporte">The Lab with Leo Laporte</a></i>, recorded in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver, British Columbia</a>, Canada. The program had formerly been known as <i><a href="/wiki/Call_for_Help_(television_program)" class="mw-redirect" title="Call for Help (television program)">Call for Help</a></i> when it was recorded in the U.S. and <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, Ontario. The series aired on <a href="/wiki/G4_(Canada)" class="mw-redirect" title="G4 (Canada)">G4 Canada</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/HOW_TO_Channel" title="HOW TO Channel">HOW TO Channel</a> in Australia, on several of Canada's <a href="/wiki/City_(TV_network)" class="mw-redirect" title="City (TV network)">Citytv</a> affiliates, and on <a href="/wiki/Google_Video" title="Google Video">Google Video</a>. On March 5, 2008, Laporte confirmed on <i>net@nite</i> that <i>The Lab with Leo Laporte</i><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> had been canceled by <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Communications" title="Rogers Communications">Rogers Communications</a>. The <a href="/wiki/HOW_TO_Channel" title="HOW TO Channel">HOW TO Channel</a> refused to air the remaining episodes after it was announced the show had been canceled.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
He hosts a weekend technology-oriented <a href="/wiki/Talk_radio" title="Talk radio">talk radio</a> program show titled <i>Leo Laporte: <a href="/wiki/The_Tech_Guy" title="The Tech Guy">The Tech Guy</a>.</i> The show, started on <a href="/wiki/KFI_AM_640" class="mw-redirect" title="KFI AM 640">KFI AM 640</a> (Los Angeles), is now syndicated through <a href="/wiki/Premiere_Radio_Networks" class="mw-redirect" title="Premiere Radio Networks">Premiere Radio Networks</a>. Laporte also appears on Friday mornings on KFI with <a href="/wiki/Bill_Handel" title="Bill Handel">Bill Handel</a>, and previously on such shows as <i><a href="/wiki/Showbiz_Tonight" title="Showbiz Tonight">Showbiz Tonight</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Live_with_Kelly" class="mw-redirect" title="Live with Kelly">Live with Kelly</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/World_News_Now" title="World News Now">World News Now</a></i>.
He holds an <a href="/wiki/Amateur_radio" title="Amateur radio">amateur radio</a> license, W6TWT.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Podcasting">Podcasting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leo_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Podcasting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Laporte owns and operates a <a href="/wiki/Podcast" title="Podcast">podcast</a> network, <a href="/wiki/TWiT.tv_(network)" class="mw-redirect" title="TWiT.tv (network)">TWiT.tv</a> with his wife<sup id="cite_ref-MarriedLisa_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MarriedLisa-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Lisa Laporte. Its shows are available on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> and other podcast subscription services. Before the expansion to new facilities in 2011, Laporte said TWiT earned $1.5 million annually on a production cost of $350,000.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In a 2012 <a href="/wiki/Reddit" title="Reddit">Reddit</a> posting, he commented that revenue was approaching $4 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Leo_Laporte_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leo_Laporte-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

The TWiT studios are located in <a href="/wiki/Petaluma,_California" title="Petaluma, California">Petaluma, California</a>, where Laporte lives. On August 21, 2016, they moved into their current facilities at 1351-B Redwood Way.<sup id="cite_ref-eastside_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastside-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The TWiT studios have always been in Petaluma, but in three different locations. TWiT identified the first as the "TWiT Cottage," the second as the "TWiT Brick House," and the current location as the "TWiT Eastside Studios."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eastside_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eastside-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
